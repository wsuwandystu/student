### Night at the Museum Summary

At the night of museum, I first arrived at the computer science room and visited 5 tables and gave 4 of them feedback. 

Computer Science:

<img src="{{site.baseurl}}/images/blog/N@m.jpg.jpg" alt= "Image 1">

Table 1:
This table was a travel planner to New York and they showed me how they made their planner. They told me how their website, updated the planner in real time to the information and date you put in at the beginning. 
The planner included:
    - Places to eat
    - Places to visit
    - Places to shop
    - Broadway shows
The feedback I gave them was to make a way to be able to add multiple things on a category without having to open a new planner.


Table 2:
This table, I found, was my favorite out of all the ones I visited because their game had a very interesting concept of a "computer science" chutes and ladders game. From this game, their boss fight part was very fun with multiple player classes to choose from. I chose their archer class, however I feel like in the boss fight, it was a bit too powerful. My feedback was simply just to nerf the character by adding a cooldown between shots, that way it both makes the game feels more natural and more of a challenge.


Table 3:
This table I found had a good idea but I was unsure of what feedback I could give them. They had a camera connected to a website and an extension that grows a plant when you are on websites that may be "on task" and shrinks when you are off task. 


Table 4:
This table was a cryptomining game, however I found it didn't seem finished and the only feedback I gave them was to add hitboxes to buildings for the character by making the buildings a seperate sprite. 


Table 5: 
This table's idea was also very creative and I felt they did a great job with the sprite's and whatnot. Although I didn't play all the games the code breaker game I played seemed well made and I saw other people play the other minigames they made. My feedback, I believe, was to add an icon on the leaderboard to show the type of currency.


After these tables, I left the class and visited other classes and sat down to watch the jazz band rehearse and than left the school satisfied for the day.
